Step 1:
Image Description: A skillet with ground beef browning over medium heat. The beef is breaking apart into small crumbles as it cooks, turning from pink to brown. Excess fat is being drained from the skillet.
Action: In a skillet, brown 1 pound of ground beef over medium heat until fully cooked, drain the excess fat *

Step 2:
Image Description: Chopped onion and bell pepper are added to the skillet with the cooked ground beef. The vegetables are sautéing, softening, and blending with the browned beef, adding texture and color to the mixture.
Action: Add 1 chopped onion and 1 chopped bell pepper to the skillet and sauté until the vegetables are tender *

Step 3:
Image Description: BBQ sauce and ketchup are being poured into the skillet with the beef and vegetables. The sauces blend into the mixture, creating a rich, tangy, and slightly sweet coating over the beef.
Action: Stir in 1 cup of your favorite BBQ sauce and 1/2 cup of ketchup *

Step 4:
Image Description: Worcestershire sauce, brown sugar, garlic powder, salt, and pepper are added to the mixture. The spices and sugar dissolve into the sauce, enhancing the flavor with a mix of sweetness and savory depth.
Action: Add 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce, 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, 1 teaspoon of garlic powder, and salt and pepper to taste *

Step 5:
Image Description: The sloppy joe mixture is simmering in the skillet, with bubbles forming as the flavors meld together. The sauce is thickening and coating the ground beef evenly, becoming richer and more flavorful.
Action: Simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld together *

Step 6:
Image Description: A spoonful of the sloppy joe mixture is being placed onto the bottom half of a soft hamburger bun. The mixture is thick and saucy, ready to be topped with the other half of the bun.
Action: Spoon the mixture onto hamburger buns *

Step 7:
Image Description: A plate with assembled sloppy joes, the buns filled with the rich, flavorful BBQ beef mixture. The sloppy joes are hot, slightly messy.
Action: Serve hot and enjoy your Sloppy BBQ Joes! *
