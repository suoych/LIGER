Step 1:

Image Description: A sharp knife slices thin strips of beef sirloin, creating uniform pieces that are perfect for quick cooking.
Action: Thinly slice 1 pound of beef sirloin. *

Step 2:

Image Description: In a mixing bowl, soy sauce, sugar, sesame oil, sake (optional), minced garlic, and minced ginger are combined, creating a fragrant and flavorful marinade.
Action: In a bowl, combine 1/4 cup soy sauce, 2 tablespoons sugar, 2 tablespoons sesame oil, 2 tablespoons sake (optional), 1 tablespoon minced garlic, and 1 tablespoon minced ginger. *

Step 3:

Image Description: The thinly sliced beef is added to the bowl of marinade, and the meat is mixed thoroughly to ensure it is evenly coated with the flavorful sauce.
Action: Add the sliced beef to the marinade and mix well to coat. *

Step 4:

Image Description: The bowl of marinated beef is covered with plastic wrap and placed in the refrigerator, allowing the flavors to infuse the meat for at least 30 minutes.
Action: Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes. *

Step 5:

Image Description: A large skillet or grill pan is heated over medium-high heat, preparing for the beef to be cooked. The surface shimmers, indicating the pan is hot enough for searing.
Action: Heat a large skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. *

Step 6:

Image Description: The marinated beef is placed into the hot pan in small batches, sizzling as it cooks. Each piece is browned and cooked through without overcrowding the pan.
Action: Cook the marinated beef in batches, without crowding the pan, until the meat is browned and cooked through. *

Step 7:

Image Description: The cooked beef bulgogi is served on a plate alongside fluffy steamed rice and a side of tangy kimchi, creating a flavorful and balanced meal.
Action: Serve the beef bulgogi with steamed rice and kimchi. *
