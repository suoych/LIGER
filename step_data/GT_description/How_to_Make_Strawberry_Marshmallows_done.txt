Step 1:

Image Description: All the ingredients are laid out, including fresh strawberry puree, packages of unflavored gelatin, granulated sugar, light corn syrup, water, confectioners’ sugar, and a pinch of salt.
Action: Prepare ingredients: 1/2 cup fresh strawberry puree, 3 packages unflavored gelatin, 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar, 1 cup light corn syrup, 1/2 cup water, confectioners’ sugar, and a pinch of salt. *

Step 2:

Image Description: A 9x13 inch baking pan is lined with parchment paper, and the bottom is generously dusted with confectioners’ sugar to prevent sticking.
Action: Line a 9x13 inch baking pan with parchment paper and dust it generously with confectioners’ sugar. *

Step 3:

Image Description: In a mixing bowl, fresh strawberry puree is combined with unflavored gelatin. The mixture is left to sit as the gelatin blooms and thickens.
Action: In a mixing bowl, combine 1/2 cup of fresh strawberry puree and the unflavored gelatin. Let it sit for 10 minutes to bloom. *

Step 4:

Image Description: In a saucepan, granulated sugar, light corn syrup, and water are combined over medium heat. The sugar is dissolving as it is stirred.
Action: In a saucepan, mix granulated sugar, light corn syrup, and water. Place it over medium heat and stir until the sugar dissolves completely. *

Step 5:

Image Description: The heat is increased to high, and a candy thermometer is clipped to the side of the saucepan. The sugar mixture is boiling and reaches 240°F, the soft-ball stage.
Action: Increase the heat to high and cook without stirring until the mixture reaches 240°F on a candy thermometer. *

Step 6:

Image Description: The saucepan is removed from the heat, and the hot syrup is slowly poured into the gelatin mixture while a mixer stirs on low speed, combining the ingredients carefully.
Action: Remove the saucepan from heat and slowly pour the hot syrup into the gelatin mixture, while mixing on low speed. *

Step 7:

Image Description: The mixer speed is increased to high, and the mixture is whipped until it becomes thick and fluffy, cooling to lukewarm over the course.
Action: Increase the mixer speed to high and whip until the mixture becomes thick and cools to lukewarm, about 10-15 minutes. *

Step 8:

Image Description: The thick marshmallow mixture is poured into the prepared baking pan, and a spatula spreads it evenly across the pan.
Action: Pour the marshmallow mixture into the prepared baking pan and spread evenly. *

Step 9:

Image Description: The top of the marshmallow mixture is dusted generously with confectioners’ sugar, and it is left to set at room temperature.
Action: Dust the top with more confectioners’ sugar and let it set at room temperature for at least 4 hours or overnight. *

Step 10:

Image Description: The fully set marshmallow slab is lifted out of the baking pan by holding the edges of the parchment paper, keeping the slab intact.
Action: Once set, lift the marshmallow slab out of the pan using the parchment paper. *

Step 11:

Image Description: A knife is used to cut the marshmallow slab into small, even squares. Each side of the marshmallow squares is dusted with confectioners’ sugar to prevent sticking.
Action: Cut the marshmallow slab into squares, and dust each side of the squares with more confectioners’ sugar to prevent sticking. *

Step 12:

Image Description: The strawberry marshmallow squares are stored in an airtight container. They are soft, fluffy, and bursting with strawberry flavor.
Action: Store the strawberry marshmallows in an airtight container. Enjoy! *
