Step 1:
Image Description: Two 9-inch round cake pans are greased and floured, ready for the batter. The oven is preheating to 350°F (175°C), and the pans are placed aside.
Action: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans *

Step 2:
Image Description: In a large bowl, the cake flour, cocoa powder, and salt are sifted together. The dry ingredients are light and powdery, creating a smooth base for the batter.
Action: In a large bowl, sift together 2 1/2 cups of cake flour, 2 tablespoons of cocoa powder, and 1 teaspoon of salt *

Step 3:
Image Description: In a separate bowl, granulated sugar and unsalted butter are creamed together. The mixture becomes light and fluffy as the sugar dissolves into the butter.
Action: In another bowl, cream together 1 1/2 cups of granulated sugar and 1/2 cup of unsalted butter *

Step 4:
Image Description: Two eggs are added one at a time into the creamed sugar and butter mixture. The eggs are fully incorporated, creating a smooth, rich base.
Action: Add 2 large eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition *

Step 5:
Image Description: Buttermilk, red food coloring, vanilla extract, and white vinegar are stirred into the mixture. The ingredients blend together, creating a vibrant red liquid base for the cake.
Action: Stir in 1 cup of buttermilk, 2 tablespoons of red food coloring, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and 1 teaspoon of white vinegar *

Step 6:
Image Description: The dry ingredients are gradually added to the wet mixture. The batter is stirred until just combined, creating a smooth and rich red cake batter.
Action: Gradually add the sifted dry ingredients to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined *

Step 7:
Image Description: The red cake batter is evenly divided between the two prepared cake pans, filling each pan to the same level.
Action: Divide the batter evenly between the two prepared cake pans *

Step 8:
Image Description: The two cake pans are placed into the preheated oven. The batter rises and bakes, turning into light, spongy cake layers.
Action: Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean *

Step 9:
Image Description: The baked cake layers are cooling in the pans, then transferred to a wire rack to cool completely. The cakes are firm.
Action: Allow the cakes to cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then remove to a wire rack to cool completely *

Step 10:
Image Description: In a medium bowl, cream cheese, unsalted butter, and powdered sugar are whipped together, forming a smooth, creamy frosting. The frosting is light and spreadable.
Action: In a medium bowl, beat together 8 oz of cream cheese, 1/2 cup of unsalted butter, and 4 cups of powdered sugar until smooth to make the frosting *

Step 11:
Image Description: One of the cooled cake layers is placed on a serving plate, and a layer of cream cheese frosting is spread evenly over the top. The second layer is placed on top, and the entire cake is frosted with the remaining frosting.
Action: Place one cake layer on a serving plate, spread frosting on top, then place the second cake layer on top and frost the entire cake *

Step 12:
Image Description: The fully assembled and frosted red velvet cake is decorated as desired. It’s ready to be sliced and served, with the vibrant red color and creamy frosting providing a classic look.
Action: Decorate as desired and serve your red velvet cake *
