Step 1:
Image Description: The smoker is preheating to 225°F (107°C), with smoke lightly rising from the vents as the temperature gauge steadily climbs.
Action: Preheat your smoker to 225°F (107°C) *

Step 2:
Image Description: A pork tenderloin is on a cutting board, being carefully trimmed with a sharp knife. The excess fat and silver skin are removed, leaving a clean, smooth piece of meat.
Action: While the smoker is preheating, trim any excess fat and silver skin from the pork tenderloin *

Step 3:
Image Description: The pork tenderloin is generously seasoned with a dry rub, covering its surface with a mix of spices and herbs. The rub adds a rich, flavorful coating to the meat.
Action: Season the pork tenderloin with a dry rub of your choice *

Step 4:
Image Description: The seasoned pork tenderloin is placed on the smoker rack, ready to absorb the smoky flavor as it cooks slowly. The rack is clean and positioned inside the smoker.
Action: Place the seasoned pork tenderloin on the smoker rack *

Step 5:
Image Description: A meat thermometer is inserted into the thickest part of the pork tenderloin, ensuring the internal temperature can be accurately monitored as it smokes.
Action: Insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the tenderloin *

Step 6:
Image Description: The pork tenderloin is smoking inside the smoker, surrounded by gentle wisps of smoke. The thermometer shows the temperature rising slowly as the tenderloin cooks evenly.
Action: Smoke the pork tenderloin until it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C), about 1.5 to 2 hours *

Step 7:
Image Description: The fully cooked pork tenderloin is removed from the smoker, with a golden, slightly charred exterior. It rests on a cutting board.
Action: Remove the pork tenderloin from the smoker and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing *
