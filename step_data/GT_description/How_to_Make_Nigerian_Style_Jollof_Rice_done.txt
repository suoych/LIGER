Step 1:
Image Description: A large pot sits over medium heat with vegetable oil sizzling.
Action: Heat 1/4 cup of vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat *

Step 2:
Image Description: Chopped onions are added to the pot, being sautéed until translucent. The onions soften and become slightly golden as they cook.
Action: Sauté 1 chopped onion until translucent *

Step 3:
Image Description: Two minced garlic cloves are added to the pot with the sautéed onions. The garlic is fragrant, and both ingredients cook together for another minute.
Action: Add 2 minced garlic cloves and sauté for another minute *

Step 4:
Image Description: A can of tomato paste is added to the onions and garlic. The ingredients are stirred together until they form a rich, combined mixture.
Action: Add 1 can of tomato paste and stir until well combined *

Step 5:
Image Description: A blended mixture of tomatoes, bell peppers, and one habanero pepper are added to the pot. The vibrant red mixture combines with the tomato paste, creating the base of the sauce.
Action: Add 2 cups of blended tomatoes, bell peppers, and one habanero pepper mixture *

Step 6:
Image Description: The tomato and pepper mixture is cooked, stirring occasionally as the sauce thickens and flavors develop.
Action: Cook for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally *

Step 7:
Image Description: Chicken broth, along with seasonings like salt, pepper, thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves, is added to the cooked sauce, enriching the flavor and creating a more liquid consistency.
Action: Add 2 cups of chicken broth and season with salt, pepper, thyme, curry powder, and bay leaves *

Step 8:
Image Description: The pot is brought to a boil, then the heat is reduced to a gentle simmer as the flavors meld together. The sauce continues to cook and thicken.
Action: Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer *

Step 9:
Image Description: Two cups of parboiled long-grain rice are added to the simmering sauce, absorbing the flavors and beginning to cook in the broth and tomato mixture.
Action: Add 2 cups of parboiled long-grain rice *

Step 10:
Image Description: The rice is stirred into the sauce and the pot is covered, cooking on low heat for 20-30 minutes as the rice becomes tender and absorbs the liquid.
Action: Stir and cover the pot, letting it cook on low heat for about 20-30 minutes *

Step 11:
Image Description: Mixed vegetables and cooked protein, like chicken or shrimp, are added to the pot of almost-cooked rice, adding color and extra nutrition to the dish.
Action: Add mixed vegetables and cooked protein (chicken or shrimp) *

Step 12:
Image Description: The rice, vegetables, and protein are stirred together. The pot is covered again, and the mixture is steamed to fully incorporate all the ingredients.
Action: Stir well, cover, and let it steam for another 10 minutes *

Step 13:
Image Description: The fully cooked Jollof rice is removed from the heat, sitting for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to settle before serving. The rice is fluffy and richly colored.
Action: Turn off the heat and let it sit for 10 minutes before serving *
