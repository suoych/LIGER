Step 1:
Image Description: A bowl with split red lentils submerged in water. The lentils are small and orange, slightly expanding as they soak, absorbing the water. The surface of the water is clear, with a few bubbles forming around the lentils.
Action: Rinse and soak 1 cup of split red lentils in water for about 30 minutes *

Step 2:
Image Description: The soaked lentils, now drained, are being transferred into a pot with water. The lentils have expanded slightly, and the pot is ready to be placed on the stove for cooking.
Action: Drain the lentils and transfer them to a pot with 3 cups of water *

Step 3:
Image Description: The pot of lentils and water is boiling on the stove. A bright yellow teaspoon of turmeric is being sprinkled into the boiling water, gradually coloring the mixture as it bubbles vigorously.
Action: Add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric and bring to a boil *

Step 4:
Image Description: The pot of lentils is now simmering, with the heat reduced. The water is gently bubbling, and the lentils are softening and turning a rich golden color from the turmeric.
Action: Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes until the lentils are soft *

Step 5:
Image Description: In a separate pan, oil or ghee is being heated, creating a slight shimmer on the surface. The pan is clean, and the oil is just beginning to warm.
Action: In a separate pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee *

Step 6:
Image Description: A teaspoon of cumin seeds is being added to the hot oil. The seeds sizzle and pop in the oil, releasing a fragrant aroma as they darken slightly, infusing the oil with flavor.
Action: Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them sizzle *

Step 7:
Image Description: Finely chopped onions are being sautéed in the pan with the cumin seeds. The onions are turning golden brown, softening, and releasing a sweet aroma, mixing with the sizzling cumin.
Action: Add a finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown *

Step 8:
Image Description: Chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies are being added to the pan. The mixture sizzles as it cooks, with the garlic turning fragrant and the chilies releasing a hint of spice into the sautéed onions.
Action: Add finely chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and sauté for another 2-3 minutes *

Step 9:
Image Description: A chopped tomato is added to the sautéed mixture, softening as it cooks. The oil starts to separate from the tomatoes and spices.
Action: Add a chopped tomato and cook until it is soft and the oil starts to separate from the mixture *

Step 10:
Image Description: Garam masala and red chili powder are being sprinkled into the cooked tomato mixture. The spices blend into the mixture, enhancing the color and aroma of the masala as everything cooks together.
Action: Add 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala and 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder *

Step 11:
Image Description: The cooked masala mixture is being poured into the pot of soft, golden lentils. The rich, spiced mixture blends with the lentils, creating a flavorful, aromatic dal as everything is stirred together.
Action: Pour the masala mixture into the pot of cooked lentils and stir well to combine *

Step 12:
Image Description: The pot of lentils is simmering gently, allowing the flavors of the masala and lentils to blend together. The dal has a smooth, slightly thickened consistency, with a rich golden color.
Action: Let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes to blend the flavors *

Step 13:
Image Description: The finished Tarka Dal is served hot, garnished with fresh cilantro leaves. The bright green cilantro contrasts beautifully with the golden-yellow dal, adding a fresh touch to the hearty dish.
Action: Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve hot *
