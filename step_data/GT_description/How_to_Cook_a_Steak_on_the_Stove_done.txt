Step 1:

Image Description: A raw steak sits on the countertop, resting at room temperature. The meat is bright red and slightly marbled with fat, preparing to be seasoned and cooked.
Action: Take the steak out of the refrigerator and let it come to room temperature for about 30 minutes. *

Step 2:

Image Description: The steak is evenly sprinkled with coarse salt and freshly ground black pepper on both sides, creating a flavorful crust as the seasoning clings to the meat.
Action: Season both sides of the steak with salt and pepper. *

Step 3:

Image Description: A heavy skillet is heating over high heat on the stovetop. The surface starts to shimmer, indicating it’s very hot and ready for the steak.
Action: Heat a skillet over high heat until it’s very hot. *

Step 4:

Image Description: A tablespoon of oil or butter is added to the hot skillet, immediately melting and spreading across the surface, creating a glossy layer that begins to sizzle.
Action: Add a tablespoon of oil or butter to the skillet. *

Step 5:

Image Description: The steak is placed in the hot skillet, and the oil sizzles loudly as the meat sears. The steak remains undisturbed, developing a golden-brown crust on one side.
Action: Place the steak in the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes without moving it. *

Step 6:

Image Description: The steak is flipped using tongs, revealing a beautifully browned crust. The other side begins to cook, with the sizzling continuing as the steak reaches medium-rare or your preferred doneness.
Action: Flip the steak and cook for another 3-4 minutes for medium-rare, or longer for your preferred doneness. *

Step 7:

Image Description: The cooked steak is placed on a wooden cutting board. The steak rests, with juices beginning to settle inside, ensuring a tender, flavorful bite.
Action: Use tongs to remove the steak from the skillet and let it rest on a cutting board for at least 5 minutes before slicing. *

Step 8:

Image Description: A sharp knife slices through the steak, revealing the juicy, tender interior. The slices are cut against the grain, showing the perfectly cooked meat, ready to serve.
Action: Slice the steak against the grain and serve. *
