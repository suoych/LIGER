Step 1:
Image Description: Fresh spinach leaves (palak), vibrant and green, are being washed under running water. After washing, the leaves are chopped into small pieces.
Action: Wash and chop 250g of fresh spinach leaves (palak) *

Step 2:
Image Description: The chopped spinach is being blanched in a pot of boiling water. The bright green leaves soften in the hot water before being transferred to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process.
Action: Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, then transfer to ice water *

Step 3:
Image Description: The blanched spinach is now drained and placed in a blender. The leaves are blended into a smooth, vibrant green puree, with no visible lumps or pieces remaining.
Action: Drain the spinach and blend into a smooth puree using a blender *

Step 4:
Image Description: A pan is heating up on the stove with oil. Cumin seeds is being added to the hot oil, where they sizzle and release their aromatic scent.
Action: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan and add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds *

Step 5:
Image Description: A finely chopped onion is added to the pan with the cumin seeds. The onion is being sautéed, turning golden brown and caramelized as it cooks in the hot oil.
Action: Sauté 1 finely chopped onion until golden brown *

Step 6:
Image Description: A spoonful of ginger-garlic paste is added to the golden onions, sizzling as it blends into the mixture. The aroma of the paste fills the air as it’s sautéed for another minute.
Action: Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute *

Step 7:
Image Description: Two finely chopped tomatoes are added to the onion and ginger-garlic mixture. The tomatoes begin to soften and break down, forming a rich, saucy base in the pan.
Action: Add 2 finely chopped tomatoes and cook until the tomatoes soften *

Step 8:
Image Description: The softened tomato mixture is being spiced with a teaspoon each of turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. The spices mix into the tomatoes, deepening the color and flavor of the dish.
Action: Add 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and 1 teaspoon of garam masala *

Step 9:
Image Description: The tomato and spice mixture is cooking until the oil starts to separate from the masala, indicating that the spices have fully cooked.
Action: Cook the mixture until the oil starts to separate from the masala *

Step 10:
Image Description: The vibrant green spinach puree is being poured into the pan with the cooked masala mixture. The ingredients blend together, forming a rich and colorful gravy.
Action: Add the spinach puree to the pan and mix well *

Step 11:
Image Description: Salt is being sprinkled into the spinach and masala mixture, which is now simmering gently. The gravy thickens as it simmers, releasing a rich aroma.
Action: Add salt to taste and simmer the mixture for 5-7 minutes *

Step 12:
Image Description: Soft paneer cubes are gently added to the spinach mixture. The white paneer contrasts with the green gravy, and the cubes are carefully stirred into the dish, absorbing the flavors.
Action: Add 200g of paneer cubes and gently mix them into the gravy *

Step 13:
Image Description: The palak paneer is cooking for another few minutes, allowing the paneer to heat through and absorb the flavors of the spinach gravy. 
Action: Cook for another 3-4 minutes until the paneer is heated through *

Step 14:
Image Description: The finished Palak Paneer is garnished with a drizzle of fresh cream or a small pat of butter, making the dish look rich and creamy. 
Action: Garnish with fresh cream or butter before serving *
