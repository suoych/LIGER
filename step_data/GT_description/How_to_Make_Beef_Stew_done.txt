Image Description: A cutting board with beef being cut into uniform 1-inch cubes. The pieces of beef are raw, fresh, and evenly sized.
Action: Cut 2 pounds of beef into 1-inch cubes. *

Step 2:

Image Description: The beef cubes are generously seasoned with salt and freshly ground black pepper. The seasoning is evenly distributed over the surface of each cube.
Action: Season the beef cubes with salt and pepper. *

Step 3:

Image Description: A large pot with olive oil heating over medium-high heat. The oil shimmers in the pot.
Action: In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. *

Step 4:

Image Description: The seasoned beef cubes are added to the hot oil in the pot. The beef sizzles and browns on all sides, developing a rich crust while cooking.
Action: Add the beef cubes to the pot and brown them on all sides. *

Step 5:

Image Description: The browned beef cubes are removed from the pot with tongs and set aside on a plate. The pot is left with flavorful drippings and oil residue.
Action: Remove the beef from the pot and set aside. *

Step 6:

Image Description: Chopped onion, carrots, and celery are added to the pot. The vegetables begin to sizzle and cook in the remaining oil and beef drippings, softening as they saut√©.
Action: Add 1 chopped onion, 2 chopped carrots, and 2 chopped celery stalks to the pot. *

Step 7:

Image Description: The vegetables in the pot are cooking, becoming soft and slightly golden. They start to release their flavors, blending with the beef drippings left in the pot.
Action: Cook the vegetables until they begin to soften. *

Step 8:

Image Description: Minced garlic are added to the softened vegetables, cooking to release their aroma.
Action: Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for an additional minute. *

Step 9:

Image Description: Four cups of beef broth and one cup of optional red wine are poured into the pot, combining with the vegetables and garlic. The liquid starts to bubble gently as it heats up.
Action: Pour in 4 cups of beef broth and 1 cup of red wine (optional). *

Step 10:

Image Description: The browned beef cubes are returned to the pot, settling into the liquid and vegetables. The pot now holds all the core ingredients for the stew.
Action: Return the beef to the pot. *

Step 11:

Image Description: Two tablespoons of tomato paste, one teaspoon of thyme, and two bay leaves are stirred into the pot, infusing the stew with rich flavors.
Action: Add 2 tablespoons of tomato paste, 1 teaspoon of thyme, and 2 bay leaves. *

Step 12:

Image Description: The pot comes to a boil, with bubbles rising to the surface. The heat is then reduced to a simmer, allowing the ingredients to cook slowly.
Action: Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a simmer. *

Step 13:

Image Description: The pot is covered with a lid, and the stew simmers gently on low heat. Over the next two hours, the beef becomes tender as the flavors develop.
Action: Cover and let simmer for 2 hours, or until the beef is tender. *

Step 14:

Image Description: Two diced potatoes are added to the simmering stew. The potatoes begin to cook, softening and absorbing the rich flavors of the broth.
Action: Add 2 diced potatoes and simmer for an additional 30 minutes. *

Step 15:

Image Description: A spoon tastes the stew, checking for seasoning. Salt and pepper are adjusted as needed to balance the flavors before serving.
Action: Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. *

Step 16:

Image Description: The finished beef stew is ladled into bowls, steaming hot. It is optionally garnished with freshly chopped parsley, adding a burst of color and freshness to the hearty dish.
Action: Serve hot, optionally garnished with fresh parsley. *
