Step 1:

Image Description: Fresh strawberries being washed under running water. The leaves (hulls) are removed, leaving clean and ready-to-use strawberries.
Action: Wash and hull the strawberries *

Step 2:

Image Description: The washed strawberries are gently patted dry with a paper towel, removing any excess moisture.
Action: Pat the strawberries dry with a paper towel *

Step 3:

Image Description: Each strawberry is wrapped in a small piece of red bean paste, forming a smooth ball around the strawberry, fully enclosing it in the sweet paste.
Action: Wrap each strawberry in a small piece of red bean paste, forming a ball around the strawberry *

Step 4:

Image Description: A microwave-safe bowl with glutinous rice flour and water is mixed together, creating a smooth and slightly thick batter-like consistency.
Action: In a microwave-safe bowl, mix 1 cup of glutinous rice flour and 2/3 cup of water until smooth *

Step 5:

Image Description: The bowl is covered with plastic wrap and placed in the microwave for 1 minute. The mixture starts to cook and thicken inside the microwave.
Action: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for about 1 minute *

Step 6:

Image Description: The partially cooked glutinous rice flour mixture is stirred to ensure even cooking.
Action: Stir the mixture, then microwave for another 1 minute *

Step 7:

Image Description: The glutinous rice flour mixture is now translucent and slightly sticky after a final microwave session. It is stirred to achieve a smooth, stretchy consistency.
Action: Stir again and microwave for additional 30 seconds if necessary, until the dough is translucent and slightly sticky *

Step 8:

Image Description: A work surface is dusted with cornstarch, and the sticky mochi dough is transferred onto it, preventing the dough from sticking as it cools slightly.
Action: Dust a work surface with cornstarch and transfer the mochi dough onto it *

Step 9:

Image Description: The mochi dough is cut into equal pieces using a knife or a dough cutter. Each piece is prepared for wrapping around the strawberries.
Action: Cut the mochi dough into equal pieces *

Step 10:

Image Description: Each piece of mochi dough is flattened using fingers, forming a small, thin disc that will easily wrap around the strawberry.
Action: Flatten each piece of mochi dough with your fingers *

Step 11:

Image Description: A red bean paste-wrapped strawberry is placed in the center of each flattened mochi dough disc.
Action: Place a red bean paste-wrapped strawberry in the center of each piece of mochi dough *

Step 12:

Image Description: The mochi dough is carefully stretched and folded over the strawberry, completely sealing it inside, forming a smooth and round mochi ball.
Action: Carefully stretch and fold the mochi dough over the strawberry, sealing it completely *

Step 13:

Image Description: The formed mochi balls are gently dusted to remove any excess cornstarch, revealing smooth, soft strawberry mochi.
Action: Dust off any excess cornstarch and serve the strawberry mochi *
