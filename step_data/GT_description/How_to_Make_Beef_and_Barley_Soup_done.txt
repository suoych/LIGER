Step 1:

Image Description: A large pot sits on the stove, with oil glistening as it heats over medium-high heat.
Action: In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat. *

Step 2:

Image Description: Cubes of raw beef are added to the hot oil in the pot, sizzling as they brown. The beef begins to develop a golden-brown crust as it sears on all sides.
Action: Add 1 pound of cubed beef and brown it on all sides. *

Step 3:

Image Description: The browned beef is removed from the pot with tongs or a slotted spoon and placed on a plate, leaving the flavorful drippings behind in the pot.
Action: Remove the beef and set it aside. *

Step 4:

Image Description: Chopped onions, carrots, and celery are added to the pot. The vegetables sizzle as they soften in the remaining oil and beef drippings, becoming tender and slightly golden.
Action: Add 1 chopped onion, 2 chopped carrots, and 2 chopped celery stalks to the pot, and sauté until tender. *

Step 5:

Image Description: Three minced garlic cloves are stirred into the sautéed vegetables, releasing a fragrant aroma as they cook for another minute, blending with the other vegetables.
Action: Stir in 3 minced garlic cloves and cook for another minute. *

Step 6:

Image Description: The browned beef cubes are returned to the pot, mixed into the sautéed vegetables, and ready to absorb the flavors from the next ingredients.
Action: Return the browned beef to the pot. *

Step 7:

Image Description: Eight cups of rich, dark beef broth are poured into the pot, fully covering the beef and vegetables, creating the base for the soup.
Action: Add 8 cups of beef broth. *

Step 8:

Image Description: One cup of pearl barley is added to the pot along with a bay leaf, salt, and pepper. The ingredients float on the surface, ready to be stirred into the broth.
Action: Stir in 1 cup of pearl barley, 1 bay leaf, and salt and pepper to taste. *

Step 9:

Image Description: The soup starts to boil, with bubbles rising to the surface as the barley and beef begin to cook and the flavors meld together.
Action: Bring the soup to a boil. *

Step 10:

Image Description: The heat is lowered, and the pot is covered with a lid. The soup simmers gently, allowing the barley to swell and the beef to become tender over the next hour.
Action: Lower the heat, cover, and simmer for 1 hour or until barley and beef are tender. *

Step 11:

Image Description: The bay leaf is removed from the pot, and the soup is tasted to adjust the seasoning if needed. The beef and barley are now fully cooked, creating a hearty soup.
Action: Remove the bay leaf and adjust seasoning if necessary. *

Step 12:

Image Description: The hot beef and barley soup is ladled into bowls, steaming and ready to be served. The broth is rich, with tender beef, vegetables, and perfectly cooked barley.
Action: Serve hot. *
