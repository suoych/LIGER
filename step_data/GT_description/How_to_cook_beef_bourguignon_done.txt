Step 1:

Image Description: A large slab of beef is cut into medium-sized chunks, with even pieces of meat prepared for cooking.
Action: Cut the beef into chunks. *

Step 2:

Image Description: The beef chunks are seasoned generously with salt and freshly cracked black pepper, ensuring each piece is well-coated with seasoning.
Action: Season the beef pieces with salt and pepper. *

Step 3:

Image Description: Oil shimmers in a large pot, heating over medium-high heat, ready to sear the beef.
Action: Heat oil in a large pot. *

Step 4:

Image Description: The seasoned beef chunks are added to the hot oil, sizzling as they brown on all sides. The surface of the beef turns golden brown, sealing in the flavor.
Action: Sear the beef chunks in the hot oil until browned on all sides. *

Step 5:

Image Description: The browned beef chunks are removed from the pot with tongs, leaving flavorful drippings in the bottom of the pot.
Action: Remove the beef from the pot. *

Step 6:

Image Description: Sliced onions and carrots are added to the pot, sizzling slightly as they begin to cook in the leftover drippings from the beef.
Action: Add sliced onions and carrots to the pot. *

Step 7:

Image Description: The onions and carrots are sautéed until the onions become soft and translucent, while the carrots start to soften.
Action: Saute until onions are translucent. *

Step 8:

Image Description: Minced garlic is stirred into the pot with the softened onions and carrots, releasing its aromatic fragrance as it cooks.
Action: Stir in minced garlic. *

Step 9:

Image Description: The browned beef chunks are returned to the pot, nestling in with the sautéed vegetables, ready to simmer in the rich sauce.
Action: Return the beef to the pot. *

Step 10:

Image Description: Red wine and beef stock are poured into the pot, submerging the beef and vegetables, creating a flavorful base for the stew.
Action: Pour in red wine and beef stock. *

Step 11:

Image Description: Tomato paste, thyme, and a bay leaf are added to the pot, enriching the stew with deep flavors and a hint of herbs.
Action: Add tomato paste, thyme, and a bay leaf. *

Step 12:

Image Description: The pot simmers on low heat, with the beef and vegetables slowly cooking in the flavorful broth for 2-3 hours, becoming tender and rich.
Action: Simmer on low heat for 2-3 hours. *

Step 13:

Image Description: Mushrooms are added to the simmering stew during the last part of the cooking process, absorbing the rich flavors of the broth.
Action: Add mushrooms towards the end of the cooking time. *

Step 14:

Image Description: The mushrooms have softened and become tender, adding depth and texture to the rich beef bourguignon.
Action: Cook until mushrooms are tender. *

Step 15:

Image Description: The completed beef bourguignon is served hot, garnished with a sprinkle of fresh parsley, adding a vibrant green touch to the hearty dish.
Action: Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley. *
